Role of the Legislative Branch

The Legislative Branch has the power to create laws.

Approving the laws that the government proposes is an expression of support from the House of Commons.

  • Laws give ministers the authority to act. For example, there is a law that gives your minister (or his/her delegate) the authority to act in a particular area of federal responsibility.

  • Parliament legislates for a minister to act and subsequently holds the minister to account.
