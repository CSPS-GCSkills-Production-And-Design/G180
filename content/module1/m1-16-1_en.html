Role of Judicial Branch

The Judicial Branch has the power to interpret the law and to judge whether actions are in compliance with the law, including whether or not laws are in compliance with the Constitution.

  • This branch is composed of various levels of provincial and federal courts.

  • Their independence from the Legislative and Executive Branches of the government provides a further check on its power.

  • The independence of judges is a fundamental principle of the rule of law.
