Main Steps of Setting Priorities

Select each photo to learn more about the three main steps of:

  • setting government priorities,

  • coordinating action toward fulfilling these priorities, and

  • funding them.
The Agenda

Step 1: As the leader of the government, the prime minister establishes its priorities, otherwise known as the “Government’s Agenda”, which are then communicated in the Speech from the Throne.

Mandate Letter

Step 2: Responsibility for the agenda’s various priorities is assigned to individual ministers in “Mandate Letters” from the prime minister.

The Budget

Step 3: The Budget is the plan for allocating money to deliver the agenda.
