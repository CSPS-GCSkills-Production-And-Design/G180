Majority Government

In a majority government, the term (about four years) is more predictable because the governing party’s members of Parliament will usually vote to support their party staying in power.

In practical terms, this means that a government has greater influence over the passage of bills intended to move the agenda forward.

At the mid-point in a majority government’s term, there will normally be a break in Parliament (a prorogation), followed by a new Speech from the Throne updating the government’s agenda.
