The Machinery Kicks In

The machinery of government refers to the structure and processes that define how work gets done.


As the leader of the government, it is the prime minister who makes such decisions, on the advice of the Privy Council Office.

The Machinery Kicks In
  • As a general rule, changes in machinery support the implementation of the agenda.

  • One of the first decisions the prime minister makes is how many ministers to have in the Cabinet and whom to choose for what post.

Decisions are Made
  • As the purpose of Cabinet is to make decisions in the interest of all Canadians, prime ministers tend to choose ministers who collectively represent the diversity of the country.

  • Ideally, the ministers selected are those best able to lead the initiatives found within their portfolios.

  • While it may appear that the prime minister has considerable latitude in making decisions, an important political consideration is how decisions will affect support for the government.

The Wheels Turn
  • In addition to selecting the Cabinet and establishing Cabinet committees, structural changes in the machinery of government may include adjustments to the responsibilities of departments or changes in departmental names.

  • Mandate letters are sent to ministers by the prime minister assigning responsibility for implementing the government’s agenda.

An example of a recent change in process was the public release of ministers’ mandate letters.
