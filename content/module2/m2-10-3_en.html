Minority Government

In a minority government, there is uncertainty with respect to the length of the term, because opposition parties may collectively out-vote the governing party on matters of “confidence.”

As a result, governments tend to proceed cautiously, including in their agendas only those priorities that they believe a majority of MPs will support.

In practical terms, it means that the pace of work tends to be slower.

In a minority government situation, each party is looking for an opportunity to trigger an election when it thinks it can win or at least increase its number of seats.
