Parliamentary Oversight

This process of proposing spending (by the Executive Branch) and the approval and oversight of it (by Parliament) is an example of how the separation of powers between these two branches works.

  • The Executive Branch can propose action.

  • The Legislative Branch or Parliament, can approve it; but, will then hold the Executive Branch to account.
